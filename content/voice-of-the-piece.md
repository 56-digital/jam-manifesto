## The Actual Thesis

Not "agents are good for music software."

**The distance between idea and execution is the creative problem. Always has been.**

The Daphne Oram example is one expression of it. The Max/MSP patch is one expression of it. The whole history of music technology is a series of partial solutions to this one problem: how do you get what's in your mind into physical reality with less friction?

## What This Means for the Piece

The agentic workflow isn't a productivity trick. It's a match between how creativity actually works and how the tools respond.

Thought is continuous. Traditional tools require it to be interrupted — write, look, error, fix, run, check. Each step is a context switch. Each context switch is a leak in the creative flow.

Voice → agent → output skips the interruption. The idea stays warm. The momentum stays.

## The Historical Lineage (three moments)

**Oram → Stockhausen → Now**

Each step is a step closer to the thought itself:

1. **Daphne Oram (1972):** Oscillators at different frequencies — the mathematics of interference made audible. Reading it, you hear it. Then you open a blank Max patch and start clicking. That distance.

2. **Karlheinz Stockhausen (~1950s-60s):** Composing in electronic music, you can draw hills and mountains — these become your envelopes. A new form of notation. The shape IS the sound.

3. **Now:** You don't draw the hills. You describe the Swiss Alps. You say "a slow rise, then a sharp peak, then falling like stone." The synthesizer performs what you saw.

The progression: notation → drawing → language.
